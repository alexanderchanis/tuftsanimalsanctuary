Mission Statement

Our Goals

At Tufts National Park, we strive to protect and preserve all species of animals. We would like to provide a habitat that radiates compassion. The animals at Tufts National Park range from zebra and elephants all the way to squirrels and rabbits. Keeping true to our roots, we have numerous nature trails that you can walk along to see the vast greenery and wildlife.

Our History

Tufts National Park started to try to preserve and showcase many of the world's animals. We founded the park 70 years ago in 1952. It was the first of its kind in the Sommerville area. We are proud to be one of the largest parks in the nation, committed to protecting and featuring a variety of animals.
